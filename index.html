1. Starting Your First Game

Choose a simple empire like the United Nations of Earth. Set difficulty to Cadet or Ensign. Focus on learning rather than winning.

3. Early Game Economy

Focus on Energy and Minerals. Keep your balance positive. Expand slowly but efficiently. Don't overspend on buildings early.

4. Science and Exploration

Assign scientists to ships. Survey fast. Ignore anomalies until you have more ships. Use Assist Research on planets later.

5. Empire & Species Design

Pick ethics and civics that match your playstyle. Try Materialist + Egalitarian. Use "Prosperous Unification" or "Mechanist" origin.

6. Key Buildings

  • Alloy Foundries: Turn minerals into alloys.
  • Research Labs: Boost tech but cost upkeep.
  • Commercial Zones: Generate Trade Value.
  • Refineries: Produce motes/gases/crystals.

7. Resources Explained

Key resources: Energy (money), Minerals (construction), Food (population), Alloys (ships), Consumer Goods (pops), Unity (traditions).

8. Ships & Weapons

  • Corvettes: Fast and cheap.
  • Destroyers: Anti-corvette and mid-game.
  • Lasers: Strong vs armor.
  • Kinetics: Strong vs shields.
  • Missiles: Ignore shields, vulnerable to PD.

9. Trade & Trade Value

Trade Value is collected via starbases. Converted to Energy, Unity, or Consumer Goods based on policies. Use Trade Hubs, protect routes from piracy.

11. Unity & Traditions

Unity unlocks traditions. Choose trees based on needs: Discovery (science), Supremacy (military), Expansion (growth). Full trees unlock Ascension Perks.

12. Edicts & Empire Management

Edicts are empire-wide buffs. Use Unity to activate them. Example: Map the Stars, Fortify the Border, Capacity Subsidies. Use them based on priorities.
